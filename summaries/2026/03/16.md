>
> **Discussion:** Commenters wrestle with the trade‑offs between Wayland’s integrated compositor/window‑manager design and the modularity they enjoyed under X11, with many expressing frustration that swapping a WM on Wayland requires re‑implementing a whole compositor rather than just a client process. Others defend the current Wayland model, arguing that combining compositor and WM solves tricky distributed‑systems problems such as initial window placement and heterogeneous DPI handling, and that the perceived complexity is often overstated because the kernel now handles much of the low‑level graphics work. A few participants note that remote input remains a pain point on Wayland, while others highlight the improved DPI support as a compelling reason to stay with Wayland despite its drawbacks. Overall, the thread reveals a split between those who see River’s separation as a much‑needed innovation for desktop experimentation and those who believe the existing Wayland architecture already provides sufficient flexibility when built on the right toolkits.
