The real question isn’t whether /e/OS works—it does—but whether it can survive in a world where Google’s control is so entrenched. The answer, as always, is probably no.

But the discussion is a masterclass in tech pragmatism versus idealism. Some developers, like Jedberg, push for structured documentation to capture the iterative process, while others, like 827a, dismiss it as noise. The real takeaway isn’t about the code; it’s about how we’re redefining what “writing code” means in the age of AI.

Critics argue that Apple is cannibalizing its own market by targeting budget-conscious users who might otherwise switch to Android. The cynical view is that the iPhone 17e is a stepping stone to a more fragmented iPhone lineup, where each model is tailored to a specific price point and feature set. It’s not innovation; it’s segmentation.
