**Score:** 746 | **Comments:** 258 | **ID:** 47507150

> **Article:** Wine 11 introduces a new kernel driver called ntsync that provides Windows‑like synchronization primitives, dramatically reducing the overhead of threading calls in games. Technical threads examined the new ntsync driver, explaining why a userspace futex‑based solution cannot match Windows performance and why kernel‑level synchronization is required for accurate semantics, and compared its gains to the earlier fsync/esync layers. The conversation also touched on personal feelings of imposter syndrome among developers working on high‑level CRUD apps versus low‑level systems work, and clarified that Proton is Valve’s branded build of Wine with additional patches rather than a separate project.
