## [Show HN: s@: decentralized social networking over static sites](http://satproto.org/)
**Score:** 394 | **Comments:** 205 | **ID:** 47344548

> **Project:** s@ is a decentralized social networking protocol based on static sites, where each user owns a static website storing all their data in encrypted JSON stores. The protocol uses X25519 keypairs for encryption and allows users to follow each other's feeds, which are composed into a single page by client applications. The system is designed to work with intermittent connectivity and can run on very low-power devices, though it faces key distribution and rotation challenges common to PGP-like systems.
>
> **Discussion:** The discussion centered on the recurring challenge of decentralized social networking protocols being too technical and encryption-dependent for mainstream adoption. Commenters noted that while the technical implementation is impressive, most people won't use systems that require managing cryptographic keys, especially when they lose access to their data if they get a new device. Some argued that the real barrier isn't technical difficulty but rather people's preference for having someone else handle their digital infrastructure, pointing out that even technically savvy users often choose centralized services like GitHub and Discord for convenience. The conversation also touched on whether middle-men are inherently problematic or provide valuable services, with examples like real estate agents and car dealerships being cited as both necessary and potentially replaceable depending on the market and regulations. Technical concerns were raised about browser localStorage being an unreliable storage mechanism for private keys, though others suggested simple export/import functionality could address this. The protocol's vulnerability to quantum computing attacks was also noted due to its use of publicly enumerable ciphertexts. Overall, the thread highlighted the tension between technical elegance and practical usability in decentralized systems.
