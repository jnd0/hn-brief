>
> **Discussion:** The Hacker News thread debates the practicality of VRAM-based swapping. Commenters highlight that while the idea creatively repurposes idle GPU memory, performance bottlenecks—particularly PCIe’s inability to sustain high-bandwidth transfers and the overhead of userspace mediation—make it unsuitable for most use cases. Technical insights emphasize that GPU VRAM lacks the latency and coherence of system RAM, and alternatives like ZRAM or larger RAM modules are preferred. A few users note potential benefits for laptops with soldered memory, where VRAM could temporarily alleviate swap-to-SSD latency. Skepticism about long-term viability persists, with comparisons to GPU-accelerated databases and speculation about future hardware advancements like CXL.
