## [Iroh 1.0](https://www.iroh.computer/blog/v1)
**Score:** 1131 | **Comments:** 342 | **ID:** 48542480

> **Article:** Iroh 1.0 is a Rust‑based library and protocol that uses QUIC and TLS to provide peer‑to‑peer connectivity across NATs and firewalls, addressing the “hole‑punching” problem without requiring a VPN. It identifies nodes by cryptographic keys rather than IP addresses and can fall back to public relays, while also allowing custom transports such as Tor, BLE, or LoRa. The project is open source (MIT/Apache2) and offers a paid hosted relay service for larger deployments. The announcement highlights a demo video, a p2p chat example in Rust, and a roadmap that includes support for additional transports and browser integration via WebRTC.
>
> **Discussion:** Commenters quickly compared Iroh to existing mesh‑VPN solutions like Tailscale and ZeroTier, noting that Iroh’s key‑based addressing and QUIC foundation differentiate it from IP‑centric VPNs. The project’s developer explained that Iroh embeds directly into applications, avoids a separate daemon, and supports custom transports, while the community raised concerns about reliance on the commercial relay service, potential vendor lock‑in, and the scalability of using the BitTorrent Mainline DHT for address lookup. Technical debates emerged around the lack of built‑in WebRTC or BLE support, the choice of relays versus relay IDs, and whether Iroh merely re‑implements DNS functionality. Overall, the thread mixed enthusiasm for the open‑source library and its performance claims with skepticism about business sustainability and real‑world use cases beyond niche P2P apps.
