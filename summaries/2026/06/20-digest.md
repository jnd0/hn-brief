The explanation is simple: cold start latency is low enough to be ignored, but the per‑invocation pricing model rewards frequent, tiny executions—a pattern that encourages developers to break up monolithic tasks into countless micro‑tasks. The result is a bloated architecture that’s harder to debug and maintain. The cynical engineer now treats “serverless” as a fancy synonym for “pay‑as‑you‑go on steroids,” and the joke about “no servers, just bills” has become a running gag in tech interview rooms.
