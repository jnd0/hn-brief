**Score:** 965 | **Comments:** 444 | **ID:** 48475483

>
> **Discussion:** Commenters debated why many developers instinctively reach for React or other heavyweight SPA frameworks even when a plain HTML approach would be simpler and more effective. Several pointed out that junior engineers often learn only the framework‑specific parts of the stack and never gain confidence in writing semantic HTML, setting up a basic HTTP server, or handling form validation without JavaScript, making the “HTML‑first” route feel like extra work despite its lower intrinsic complexity. The conversation also highlighted accessibility concerns, noting that older users and those on slower devices struggle with bloated JavaScript‑heavy sites, and that government or public‑service projects suffer when teams prioritize developer familiarity over user experience. A few contributors shared practical setups that combine HTMX with Go and SQLite, praising the balance of interactivity and simplicity, while others asked for guidance on managing complex forms without letting HTMX‑replaced fragments become unwieldy. Overall, the thread revealed a tension between the allure of modern tooling and the enduring benefits of progressive enhancement, with many urging a return to foundational web skills as a way to build faster, more inclusive products.
