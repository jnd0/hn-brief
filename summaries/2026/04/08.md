## [MegaTrain: Full Precision Training of 100B+ Parameter LLMs on a Single GPU](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05091)
**Score:** 326 | **Comments:** 57 | **ID:** 47689174

> **Article:** MegaTrain is a system that enables full‑precision training of language models with over 100 billion parameters on a single GPU by keeping all model parameters and optimizer states in host (CPU) memory and streaming them layer‑by‑layer to the GPU as needed. The authors demonstrate training a 100 B‑parameter model on an RTX 4090, achieving a throughput of roughly 341 tokens per second, and show that the approach works with standard optimizers such as Adam while requiring only the GPU as a transient compute engine.
>
> **Discussion:** Commenters were quick to note the practical appeal for hobbyists with limited VRAM, like an RTX 3080, but many stressed that the speed—hundreds of tokens per second on a consumer GPU—is still far too slow for any serious pre‑training effort. Several users pointed out that similar CPU‑offload tricks have existed for years, and that additional memory‑saving techniques (gradient accumulation, Muon optimizer, 4‑bit quantization, fused cross‑entropy kernels) could further improve efficiency. Skepticism arose over the paper’s claims, with some arguing that the method merely reduces data movement rather than eliminating it, and that large context windows would still run out of memory. Others highlighted that the approach may be useful for fine‑tuning or specialized tool‑adaptation scenarios, while noting that true scalability would require high‑end hardware such as NVIDIA H200 cards with terabytes of host memory. The overall tone blended enthusiasm for the idea with a realistic appraisal of its limited applicability to large‑scale training.
