The "what vs. why" comment debate is nuanced. While many agree that "why" comments (explaining business logic or non-obvious decisions) are essential, they also defend "what" comments. The distinction between "what" and "why" is seen as a false dichotomy, as business rules often embody both.

The overall sentiment is pragmatic. The community favors a balanced approach where the primary goal is to make the code understandable. Comments should be used as a tool to achieve this, without rigid rules against any specific type. The article's age is noted, but its message is considered more relevant than ever as a reaction against the dogmatism of the "Clean Code" movement.
