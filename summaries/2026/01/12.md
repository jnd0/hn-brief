## [Sampling at negative temperature](https://cavendishlabs.org/blog/negative-temperature/)
**Score:** 176 | **Comments:** 52 | **ID:** 46579374

> **Article:** The article from Cavendish Labs explores the concept of "negative temperature" in the context of Large Language Model (LLM) sampling. It explains that the standard softmax function used to generate token probabilities can be extended to negative values for the temperature parameter (T). While positive T > 1 increases randomness and T < 1 makes outputs more deterministic (favoring high-probability tokens), negative T inverts the distribution. This causes the model to sample from the *least likely* tokens. As T approaches 0 from the negative side, the output becomes deterministic but selects the most anomalous, lowest-probability tokens. The author demonstrates this with a model, showing it produces nonsensical, "alien" text, and notes that these least likely tokens often cluster near the semantic centroid of the model's vocabulary, having little distinct meaning.
