## [Amazon is ending all inventory commingling as of March 31, 2026](https://twitter.com/ghhughes/status/2012824754319753456)
**Score:** 418 | **Comments:** 233 | **ID:** 46678205

> **Article:** Amazon will end its inventory commingling program for third-party sellers as of March 31, 2026. Under commingling, Amazon stored inventory from different sellers with the same SKU together and fulfilled orders from the nearest warehouse, regardless of the seller. This practice often resulted in customers receiving counterfeit or inferior products from unknown sellers, even when purchasing from a reputable vendor. The policy change ensures that when a customer buys from a specific seller, they will receive the actual inventory sourced from that seller.
>
> **Discussion:** The Hacker News community largely welcomed the announcement, viewing it as a long-overdue correction to a system that prioritized logistics efficiency over product authenticity. Many users shared personal experiences of receiving counterfeit goods—such as defective HDMI adapters and toner cartridges—despite buying from trusted sellers, confirming that commingling facilitated fraud.

The discussion focused on the reasons behind Amazon's decision. While some speculated it was a move to rebuild consumer trust, others suggested financial pressure from manufacturers or large buyers burned by counterfeit inventory was the primary driver. Several commenters expressed skepticism that the change would restore their trust in Amazon, citing years of reputational damage and a general decline in service quality.

The conversation also touched on broader themes of platform accountability. Some users noted that the policy change highlights the inherent conflict in Amazon's business model, where the marketplace and fulfillment services blur the lines of responsibility. A parallel was drawn to the Kenyan coffee industry, where a centralized, commingled grading system historically prevented farmers from being rewarded for higher quality, suggesting that Amazon's system similarly created a "race to the bottom."
