## [Gaussian Splatting – A$AP Rocky "Helicopter" music video](https://radiancefields.com/a-ap-rocky-releases-helicopter-music-video-featuring-gaussian-splatting)
**Score:** 708 | **Comments:** 228 | **ID:** 46670024

> **Article:** The article discusses A$AP Rocky's music video for "Helicopter," which prominently features Gaussian Splatting, a novel volumetric rendering technique. Unlike traditional NeRFs (Neural Radiance Fields) which use implicit neural representations, Gaussian Splatting utilizes an explicit point cloud of millions of 3D ellipsoids (Gaussians) to represent a scene. This allows for significantly faster rendering and easier manipulation within standard VFX software like Houdini and OctaneRender. The production team scanned actors in a controlled environment and integrated these volumetric captures into the video, enabling dynamic camera movements and re-lighting that would be difficult with standard 2D compositing. The article notes that while the technology requires substantial data capture (over 10TB for 30 minutes of footage), the post-production flexibility and speed make it a viable tool for high-end music videos and film production.
>
> **Discussion:** The discussion is a mix of technical analysis, aesthetic appreciation, and casual commentary. A significant portion of the conversation focuses on clarifying the technology: users distinguish Gaussian Splatting from NeRFs, noting that while both are radiance fields, Gaussian Splatting uses an explicit point cloud rather than an inferred neural network, making it faster to render. One of the creators of GSOPs (a Houdini tool for Gaussian Splatting) even chimed in to explain the workflow.

Regarding the video's visual style, there is a debate on whether the "low-fi," slightly surreal look was an intentional aesthetic choice or a limitation of the technology. Some commenters felt it looked like an old version of Unreal Engine, while others argued that the artifacts of dynamic Gaussian Splatting were leaned into intentionally to create a frenetic, surreal energy. The consensus was that the flexibility of the tech—allowing for re-lighting and easy camera manipulation—justified its use despite the high resource requirements (10TB of data).

There was also a meta-discussion about the relevance of the post on Hacker News, with users surprised to see a mainstream music artist featured alongside deep technical discussion. Finally, some users questioned the practical efficiency compared to traditional methods like drone filming, to which others replied that the creative flexibility and the "cool factor" of the novel technology were the primary drivers.
