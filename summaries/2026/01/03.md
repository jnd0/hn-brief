## [Publish on your own site, syndicate elsewhere](https://indieweb.org/POSSE#)
**Score:** 642 | **Comments:** 152 | **ID:** 46468600

> **Article:** The article (linked from the Indieweb wiki) introduces the POSSE (Publish on your Own Site, Syndicate Elsewhere) strategy. This involves creating content on a personal website that you control, and then cross-posting (syndicating) it to various social media platforms like Twitter, Mastodon, or Substack. The core philosophy is to maintain ownership and a "single source of truth" for your content, while still engaging with audiences on centralized platforms. It is presented as an alternative to PESOS (Publish Elsewhere, Syndicate to your Own Site), where content is created on third-party platforms and then aggregated back to a personal site.
>
> **Discussion:** The Hacker News community largely embraced the POSSE philosophy, viewing it as a return to the original, decentralized ideals of the web. A central theme was the importance of **ownership and control**. Commenters noted that POSSE ensures the author owns their content and has a single source of truth, contrasting this with platforms like Facebook that can down-rank syndicated content or claim ownership. The discussion also highlighted the practicalities of implementation, with users sharing their personal workflows (e.g., posting to a personal site first, then manually or automatically syndicating to Mastodon, Bluesky, and Twitter) and mentioning tools like PosseParty that can automate the process by using an Atom feed.

Beyond the technical, the conversation touched on the broader implications of this approach. Some argued that this model is a way to "disrupt" the marketplace of attention, where users are the product. While there was a nostalgic sentiment for a more open web built on standards like RSS and microformats, some users offered counterpoints, suggesting that a complete rejection of modern platforms is impractical and that the goal should be to coexist and leverage these networks rather than retreat from them entirely. The discussion ultimately framed POSSE as a pragmatic strategy for navigating the current centralized internet while preserving personal autonomy.

*   **Saving & Configuring:** Using `s` to save the current pipe or file content to a new file, and using a `~/.lesskey` file to customize key bindings.
