Even long-time Linux users admit the platform isn't flawless.
*   `marginalia_nu` highlighted persistent issues like UI framework fragmentation (GTK vs. Qt, X11 vs. Wayland), poor support for non-standard DPI monitors, and the difficulty of configuring audio and webcams.

In essence, the discussion portrays a tipping point where user frustration with Microsoft's decisions (licensing, hardware requirements, performance) is overcoming the historical friction of switching to Linux, with gaming viability being a key battleground.
