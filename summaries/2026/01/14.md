## [Chromium Has Merged JpegXL](https://chromium-review.googlesource.com/c/chromium/src/+/7184969)
**Score:** 408 | **Comments:** 136 | **ID:** 46597927

> **Article:** The article reports that Chromium (the open-source project behind Chrome, Edge, and other browsers) has officially merged support for the JPEG XL image format. This is a significant reversal of a 2021 decision by Google to remove JPEG XL support from Chrome, which had been a point of controversy. The merge is considered a major step toward making JPEG XL a viable, web-wide standard.
>
> **Discussion:** The Hacker News discussion is overwhelmingly positive about the news, viewing it as a long-overdue correction. The consensus is that JPEG XL is a technically superior format to the current standards, offering better compression than both JPEG and WebP, and more reasonable encoding speeds than AVIF.

Key points of discussion include:

*   **Technical Superiority:** Users cite benchmarks showing JPEG XL provides better compression at similar quality levels compared to WebP and JPEG. It's also praised for its versatility, supporting both lossy and lossless compression, animations, and high-dynamic-range (HDR) in a single format, potentially replacing the need for both JPEG and PNG.
*   **The "Rust" Factor:** A major reason for the previous reluctance and current acceptance is the implementation language. The original C++ JPEG XL library was seen as a security risk due to being unmaintained. The new, accepted implementation is written in Rust, which provides memory safety guarantees and alleviates many security concerns.
*   **Performance Trade-offs:** While AVIF offers slightly better compression, it is criticized for being extremely slow to encode. Users see JPEG XL as a better "Pareto-optimal" choice, offering excellent compression with fast encoding speeds, making it more practical for widespread use.
*   **Adoption and Ecosystem:** Commenters acknowledge that adoption will be slow, mirroring the decade-long rollout of WebP. However, they note that Chromium's support is the critical first step, as it creates a baseline for web developers to target. The lack of a freely available specification is mentioned as a minor concern.
*   **Practicality:** Some users express frustration with new formats due to poor software support outside of browsers (e.g., in image editors), but others point out that WebP is now universally supported and that GIFs should be replaced by video formats for efficiency.
