> **Article:** The UK government has abandoned plans to make a single, mandatory digital ID a requirement for working in the country. The original proposal, part of a broader plan to streamline the economy, faced opposition over concerns about privacy, cost, and feasibility. However, the government will still proceed with making digital right-to-work checks mandatory by 2029, using existing documents like biometric passports. This move is seen as a compromise, shifting focus from creating a new centralized ID system to digitizing the verification of existing credentials.
