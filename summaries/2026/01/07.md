## [High-Performance DBMSs with io_uring: When and How to use it](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.04859)
**Score:** 143 | **Comments:** 38 | **ID:** 46517319

> **Article:** The paper "High-Performance DBMSs with io_uring: When and How to use it" provides a practical guide for database architects on leveraging the Linux kernel's `io_uring` for significant performance gains. The authors argue that `io_uring` is not a simple drop-in replacement for traditional synchronous I/O calls but requires a fundamental architectural shift. The key benefits stem from its unified interface for both storage and network I/O, asynchronous nature, and efficient batching of operations.

The paper outlines a step-by-step approach: first, confirm I/O is the bottleneck; second, redesign the application for async/batching; and only then, adopt advanced `io_uring` features like fixed buffers, zero-copy, and polling. It highlights that a "thread-per-core" model, where each thread has its own ring to overlap computation and I/O, is the ideal design pattern. For maximum performance, the paper details advanced configurations, such as using IOPOLL for storage (which polls device queues and disables non-polled I/O, often necessitating a separate ring for network traffic) and SQPOLL for automated submission, noting that these features offer a trade-off between latency and CPU usage.
>
> **Discussion:** The Hacker News discussion was overwhelmingly positive, with readers praising the paper for its clarity and practical value. A co-author's presence fostered direct engagement. Key themes in the discussion included:

*   **Clarifications on Advanced Features:** A detailed exchange clarified the nuances of `io_uring`'s high-performance modes.
