## [Finding and fixing Ghostty's largest memory leak](https://mitchellh.com/writing/ghostty-memory-leak-fix)
**Score:** 235 | **Comments:** 56 | **ID:** 46568794

> **Article:** The article by Mitchell Hashimoto details the discovery and fix of a significant memory leak in the Ghostty terminal emulator's scrollback buffer. The leak was caused by a mismatch between the memory allocation size for "standard" and "non-standard" scrollback pages. When a non-standard page (e.g., containing UTF-8 characters that require more memory) was pruned from the buffer, the system incorrectly tried to reuse its memory block for a new standard-sized page. This led to the original, larger memory block being lost, causing a memory leak. The fix was simple: prevent the reuse of non-standard pages, ensuring they are always freed and only standard-sized pages are recycled.
>
> **Discussion:** The Hacker News discussion praised the article's accessibility and the value of a reliable reproduction for fixing the bug. A key point of clarification from the author, mitchellh, was that the bug was extremely rare until the recent rise of Claude Code, which increased its occurrence. He also defended his initial cautious response to bug reports by noting the lack of a reproducible case, which was only provided recently.

Technical discussion centered on the simplicity of the fix. One user proposed more complex alternative solutions (e.g., tagging memory blocks), but another commenter pointed out that the actual four-line fix was far simpler and less complex than the proposed alternatives. The author also explained that the chosen architecture (a linked list of blocks) was to avoid large memory allocations or copies that a simple circular buffer might entail. Overall, the community was positive, viewing the fix as pragmatic and well-explained.
