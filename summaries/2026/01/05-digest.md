The HN discussion crystallizes the core architectural schism—critics argue Wayland's protocol-as-display-server model fragments the ecosystem and shifts complexity to compositors, while defenders insist its modern stack (xdg-desktop-portal, etc.) is fundamentally sound. Pragmatists note that regardless of technical merit, Wayland is becoming the de facto standard simply because it's where active development lives, leaving power users to wrestle with workflow rewrites or stubbornly cling to Xorg's "tried and true" quirks.
