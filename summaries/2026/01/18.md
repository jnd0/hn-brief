The visual argument is that as icons have evolved, they have lost clarity and meaning, becoming "better at design" in an artistic sense but worse at being functional icons.
>
> **Discussion:** The commenters largely disagreed with the premise that modern icons are superior, with a strong consensus that the middle-ground of the timeline offered the best balance of clarity and branding. Several distinct themes emerged in the debate:

**Functionality vs. Artistry**
The dominant opinion was that icon design prioritizes clarity and affordance over pure illustration. While the earliest icons were criticized for being too illustrative and the latest for being too abstract, the middle iterations were praised for being recognizable, distinct, and clear. Commenters argued that effective icons must be understood in the context of a UI, not as standalone art.

**The Skeuomorphism vs. Flat Design Debate**
Users expressed frustration with the current trend of "flat" or minimalist design. Skeuomorphism advocates argued that realistic icons are objectively easier to understand and cited the lack of intuitive meaning in modern abstract shapes (e.g., a rectangle with a line). Conversely, others noted that while skeuomorphism is clear, it can feel dated, whereas flat design aims for timelessness, even if it sometimes fails to convey meaning immediately.

**Cognitive Load and Memorability**
A recurring point was the difficulty of distinguishing between modern icons that rely on similar shapes and color palettes (specifically citing Google's design language). Users noted that older, more distinct icons allowed for faster recognition, whereas modern icons often require closer inspection or rely on color coding that can be unpredictable or fail in monochromatic contexts.

**Design Process and User Expectation**
One commenter offered a theoretical perspective, suggesting that good icons act as an "attractor" in a phase space of user expectations—meaning an icon doesn't need to look like the object it represents, but must consistently evoke the correct concept in the user's mind. Another shared a personal anecdote about the difficulty of designing icons for a complex app, highlighting the tension between stakeholder opinions and the need for a unified, user-friendly visual language.
