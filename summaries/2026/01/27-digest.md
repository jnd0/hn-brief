The most significant story of the day is a sobering look at the U.S. government's accelerating brain drain, with over 10,000 STEM PhDs leaving federal service since the current administration took office. The Hacker News discussion framed this not merely as a personnel issue, but as a geopolitical pivot point. The consensus among commenters is that the U.S. is voluntarily ceding scientific leadership, with a European researcher noting that the resulting research void is being eagerly filled by China, which has the funding and political will to advance. While a minority argued this could be a market correction for a broken academic system producing low-quality research, the overwhelming sentiment was one of self-inflicted decline, with the most talented simply finding opportunities elsewhere.
