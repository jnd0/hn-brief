## [LLMs could be, but shouldn't be compilers](https://alperenkeles.com/posts/llms-could-be-but-shouldnt-be-compilers/)
**Score:** 94 | **Comments:** 107 | **ID:** 46912781

> **Article:** The article argues that while large language models (LLMs) are technically capable of translating natural‑language specifications into executable code, they should not be treated as compilers. It points out that LLMs are fundamentally stochastic, often produce hallucinations, and lack the semantic closure that guarantees a compiler’s output is always well‑defined and verifiable. The author also notes that even with deterministic sampling (e.g., temperature set to 0 and a fixed seed), the underlying interface remains underspecified, making reliable compilation impossible. Consequently, the piece cautions against relying on LLMs as a replacement for traditional compilation pipelines.
>
> **Discussion:** Commenters quickly converged on nondeterminism as a core obstacle, with codingdave emphasizing that LLMs’ stochastic nature disqualifies them as compilers, while CGMthrowaway countered that determinism isn’t required—what matters is semantic closure, which LLMs lack. A secondary theme emerged around reproducibility: users like moregrist and thwarted argued that compilers must produce identical binaries for the same inputs, a guarantee LLM‑generated code cannot provide. Several participants, such as rvz and WithinReason, debated whether deterministic sampling (fixed seeds, temperature 0) resolves the issue, with hackinthebochs noting that any injected randomness is artificial but the model’s behavior remains unpredictable. Practical concerns were raised by raw_anon_1111 and skydhash, who compared debugging LLM‑generated code to traditional compiled code, highlighting that developers still need to verify correctness and that performance‑critical scenarios expose the limits of LLM outputs. Overall, the community expressed skepticism, acknowledging LLMs’ utility for prototyping but rejecting them as a reliable, standards‑compliant compilation tool.
