The broader discussion was less about whether Ruby should replace Bash, and more about the desire to fuse language ecosystems in ways that break the traditional siloing of tools. In a world where Docker containers have made the “one‑shell‑to‑rule‑them‑all” myth a hard‑sell, these projects remind us that there’s still a subculture that values clever language mash‑ups over raw speed. It’s a microcosm of the larger debate about what “efficiency” really means in the age of cloud‑native workloads.

While the transition promises better safety guarantees and a smaller attack surface, the comments were laced with the usual cynicism: “Will the ecosystem catch up?
