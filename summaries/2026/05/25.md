The authors report a consistent “constraint decay” effect: while models achieve a 78 % pass@1 rate on unconstrained code synthesis, their success drops to roughly 45 % when the same tasks must also satisfy explicit structural rules. Experiments focus on dynamically typed languages (Python and JavaScript) and show that even modest increases in non‑functional requirements (e.g., linting or type‑checking) cause a steep performance decline.
