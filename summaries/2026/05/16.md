## [Orthrus-Qwen3: up to 7.8×tokens/forward on Qwen3, identical output distribution](https://github.com/chiennv2000/orthrus)
**Score:** 161 | **Comments:** 25 | **ID:** 48154865

> **Article:** Orthrus-Qwen3 is a method that injects a trainable diffusion attention module into each layer of a frozen autoregressive transformer like Qwen3, sharing a single KV cache between the diffusion head and the original AR head. The diffusion head generates 32 tokens in parallel, and the AR head verifies them in a second pass, accepting the longest matching prefix—resulting in a provably identical output distribution to the base model. The technique achieves up to 7.8× tokens per forward pass and roughly 6× wall-clock speedup on MATH-500, while only training 16% of parameters on 1B tokens for 24 hours using 8× H200 GPUs. Unlike prior diffusion LMs that modify base weights and lose accuracy, Orthrus freezes the backbone, so accuracy exactly matches Qwen3-8B, and it adds only ~4.5 MiB of flat KV overhead.
>
> **Discussion:** Commenters immediately recognized Orthrus as a clever variant of speculative decoding that effectively batches token verification within the same model, eliminating the need for an external drafter or separate cache. A detailed explanation emerged: autoregressive LLMs are memory-latency-bound at low batch sizes, so generating multiple tokens in parallel—and discarding mismatched ones—dramatically improves throughput even though total compute increases slightly. Several users asked about porting the approach to other models like Qwen 3.6 27B, quantized GGUF files, and local inference setups; responses suggested it is feasible but non-trivial due to architectural differences and training costs. There was a minor debate about when compute actually becomes the bottleneck—for large-context agentic workloads, memory transfer (especially KV-cache) remains the limit. The community was broadly enthusiastic, calling the results “fantastic” and praising the insight of sharing the KV cache, though some noted that a full diffusion transformer could be faster if quality degradation were acceptable, and that the method inherits all limitations of the frozen base model (biases, hallucinations, knowledge gaps).
