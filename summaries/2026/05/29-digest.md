The thread highlighted a paradox: while the financial upside of data sales is surprisingly modest, the regulatory inertia and corporate lobbying create a landscape where privacy feels like a theoretical construct rather than a practical reality.

The forum’s reaction split between outrage at corporate overreach and pragmatic acceptance that security measures, while perhaps excessive, are becoming the new norm; a minority attempted to inject historical grievances about Volkswagen’s Nazi‑era roots into the debate, a move that was swiftly rebuked as irrelevant. Technical voices pointed out that the underlying cryptographic checks are already mandatory for safety‑critical CAN traffic, and the client‑assertion layer merely formalizes existing safeguards rather than introducing novel protection. Ultimately, the discussion reflected a broader unease about how proprietary control can stifle innovation in home automation while offering dubious security benefits to end users.
