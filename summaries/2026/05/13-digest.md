Ultimately, the conversation circles back to a fundamental truth: all software has bugs, but the question is whether some languages and processes mitigate risks better than others.

Benchmarks showing 76GB CSV transfers at near-maximum network throughput impressed some, though others criticized the dataset’s compression potential and encoding transparency. Critics argue HTTP is inefficient for large-scale data streaming, while proponents emphasize its practicality in modern infrastructure. The protocol’s potential to replace PostgreSQL in specific workflows sparks debate, with recommendations for Firebird or PostgreSQL for broader client-server needs. DuckDB’s evolving scope, however, raises questions about its long-term focus.
