The issue was caused by a botched ZSK (Zone Signing Key) rollover, where DENIC published an RRSIG over an NSEC3 record that didn't validate against ZSK 33834. >
> **Discussion:** The discussion centered on DNSSEC's practical value and implementation challenges, with many questioning whether the security benefits outweigh the operational complexity. Technical insights revealed the issue was specifically with malformed RRSIG signatures during a ZSK rollover, affecting all .de domains intermittently due to some instances still serving valid signatures. The conversation also highlighted broader concerns about internet fragility, centralization risks in DNS infrastructure, and the need for better disaster recovery plans for critical infrastructure, with one commenter noting that "brittle infrastructure is a political risk" at a national level.
