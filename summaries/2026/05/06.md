## [.de TLD offline due to DNSSEC?](https://dnssec-analyzer.verisignlabs.com/nic.de)
**Score:** 644 | **Comments:** 314 | **ID:** 48027897

> **Article:** The .de top-level domain experienced a major outage affecting all DNSSEC-signed domains due to a DNSSEC signature validation failure. According to commenters, DENIC published an RRSIG over an NSEC3 record that failed validation against ZSK 33834, causing validating DNS resolvers to return SERVFAIL responses for all .de domains. The issue appeared to stem from a botched zone signing key rollover, as DENIC rotates their ZSK every 5 weeks via pre-publish. Cloudflare responded by disabling DNSSEC validation on their 1.1.1.1 resolver as a workaround, and DENIC acknowledged the disruption on their status page.
>
> **Discussion:** Commenters debated whether this incident demonstrated the brittleness of internet infrastructure versus the benefits of DNSSEC. Some argued that a single configuration mistake wiping out external reachability for Germany's economy highlighted dangerous centralization risks, while others noted that DNSSEC actually makes DNS more decentralized by allowing trust in third-party caching resolvers. Technical discussions revealed the complexity of DNSSEC, with several commenters admitting they didn't understand the acronyms despite years of IT experience, and one user sharing frustrations with cryptographic standards like ASN.1 and ed25519 implementations. The community also discussed workarounds like adding "domain-insecure: de" to unbound configurations, and there was debate about whether this incident would be a significant political risk given Germany's economic importance. Some commenters made light of the situation with jokes about German bureaucracy and pessimistic time estimates, while others expressed genuine concern about the impact on millions of businesses and prominent sites like amazon.de and spiegel.de.
