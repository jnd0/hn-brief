# HN Daily Digest - 2026-05-31

Microsoft's latest move to convert Office 2019 and 2021 for Mac to view-only mode by 2026 has predictable tech commenters up in arms, though we've all seen this coming. The certificate expiration excuse feels as thin as the company's commitment to the open-source values they pay lip service to. Commenters rightly point out this is just Microsoft's latest tactic to force users toward subscription models, with some speculation that it's aimed at preventing AI labs from using licensed versions in automated workflows without proper licensing. The irony of them pushing developers toward open-source alternatives while their core business model remains extraction through lock-in isn't lost on anyone who's been paying attention for more than five years.

For those interested in hardware-level computing, "Microcode inside the Intel 8087 floating-point chip: register exchange" provided fascinating insights into the microcode that drives Intel's 8087 floating-point coprocessor. The article reveals that its operations are built from just eight identical micro-instructions, stored in compressed ROM, allowing the 8087 to run in parallel with the 8086 CPU without stalling the main processor. Commentators quickly pivoted to the broader evolution from micro-coded to RISC-style designs, with one user noting that Intel could have opted for a single-clock instruction set stored in user-accessible RAM, simplifying the architecture. A former Intel engineer recounted the daily grind of validating customer bug reports, emphasizing that many FP precision issues arose from different instruction orders yielding divergent results. The discussion touched on the tension between hardware complexity and performance, as later CPUs incorporated floating-point units directly into their instruction sets.
