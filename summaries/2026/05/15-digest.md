The futility theme isn’t just philosophical; it’s technical. Even if you gut a car’s hardware, the software can still find a way to phone home, either through the phone’s network or obscure APIs. The real kicker is that screens and driver-assist systems are now mandatory by regulation, making it impossible to buy a car that’s truly yours. The CarPlay/Android Auto exception is particularly ironic. This isn’t just a privacy issue—it’s a question of agency. How much of our behavior is being optimized for corporate interests? The comment thread’s fixation on Tesla’s software reliability versus its safety flaws feels like a collective therapy session for the disillusioned.
